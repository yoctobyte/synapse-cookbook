# A note on provenance and authenticity

This cookbook has two very different layers, and honesty demands they be labelled.

## The historical layer (2004–2008)

The recovered material under [`releases/`](releases/) — the 0.1 PDF and its
extracted text — is the *original* community wiki content, written by human hands
two decades ago and rescued verbatim from the Internet Archive. It is a genuine
historical artifact. It is also, by its author's own long-ago admission,
unfinished and only lightly checked.

## The modern layer (2026)

The revived Markdown under [`book/`](book/) — the architecture chapters and the
rest as they fill in — is **largely AI-generated**, written by a language model
reading the upstream Ararat Synapse source and composing prose about it. It is
meant to be accurate and useful, and the code claims are drawn from the real
source, but it has not been line-edited or vouched for by a human expert.

I neglected this cookbook for the better part of twenty years. Reviving it with an
AI is the honest state of things: the ideas and the library are old and human;
the new writing around them is machine-made, on my behalf, because I finally got
round to it — with help.

## Authenticity

No printed or "canonical" edition of this cookbook is authenticated by me.

I will authenticate exactly one physical copy — and only once **Lukas Gebauer**,
the author of Ararat Synapse, signs it by hand, in person. Until then, treat
everything here as useful, unofficial, and unsigned: a community tribute to a
beautiful library, not an authorized document.

Credit for Synapse itself belongs entirely to Lukas. This cookbook only admires
it out loud.
